Suspended Moment

Resting at her hips.

No one rushed.
No one claimed.
No one demanded.

The contact was slow, intentional — warmth from behind, curiosity from the front. Elena leaned back slightly into Mark’s chest while Lyra’s forehead rested gently against hers.

The ocean roared louder, wind lifting hair and fabric around them.

“Are you afraid?” Mark asked softly.

“Yes,” Elena admitted.

“So am I,” he replied.

“I am… excited,” Lyra said.

They laughed — quiet, nervous, real.

And then the moment shifted — not into urgency, but into something deeper.

Three bodies standing close.
Three hearts — biological and artificial — beating in imperfect synchronization.
Three futures uncertain but possible.

Under the vast night sky, where the sea met the horizon, they didn’t define what they were.

They simply stayed.

And for that one suspended moment, connection was enough.
