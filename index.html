Whispers After Midnight

Ethan never believed in fate. He believed in timing, in logic, in things that could be measured. Until the night she appeared online. Her username was simple. Her first message even simpler: “Can’t sleep either?” What started as a casual reply turned into hours of conversation. Music preferences. Childhood memories. Confessions shared only in darkness. He told her about the city lights outside his window. She described the rain tapping softly against her glass. Somewhere between 1:00 AM and 3:00 AM, something shifted. It wasn’t just attraction. It was recognition. “Do you ever feel,” she typed slowly, “like you’ve been waiting for someone you’ve never met?” His fingers hovered over the keyboard before he answered. “Yes. I think I just found her.” The world outside was quiet. But inside that glowing screen, something electric was building — anticipation, curiosity, desire. They weren’t rushing. They were savoring. Every word intentional. Every pause meaningful. And when she finally sent: “Maybe we shouldn’t just imagine it anymore…” His heart pounded.
